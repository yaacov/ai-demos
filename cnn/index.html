Convolutional Neural Network

CNNs are the architecture behind image recognition. LeNet (1989) by Yann LeCun was the first practical CNN, used by the USPS to read handwritten zip codes on envelopes. This demo walks through how a CNN processes a handwritten digit image step by step.

The visualization uses a simplified 12×12 pixel grid (real MNIST images are 28×28) so you can follow every computation by hand.

  • Follow the image through convolution, pooling, and classification
  • See how filters detect edges and patterns
  • Understand why CNNs work for images
