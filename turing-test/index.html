The Turing Test

In 1950, Alan Turing asked: "Can machines think?" Rather than debate philosophy, he proposed a practical test: a human judge converses with two hidden entities — one human, one machine — through text only. If the judge can't reliably tell which is which, the machine passes the test.

This simulation puts you in the judge's seat across three eras of AI: a rule-based expert system, a small 2B-parameter model, and a modern frontier LLM. How hard is it to tell which is the machine?

  • Compare three different AI systems against human conversations
  • Look for telltale signs — highlights reveal clues after your verdict
  • See how AI has evolved from trivially detectable to genuinely challenging
