The Turing Test

In 1950, Alan Turing asked: "Can machines think?" Rather than debate philosophy, he proposed a practical test: a human judge converses with two hidden entities — one human, one machine — through text only. If the judge can't reliably tell which is which, the machine passes the test.

This interactive simulation puts you in the judge's seat. You'll read parallel conversations and try to identify which entity is the machine.

  • Read exchanges from two conversations side by side
  • Look for telltale signs — hover over highlights for clues
  • Make your verdict: which conversation is the machine?
