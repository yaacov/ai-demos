Turing Machine

In 1936, Alan Turing imagined the simplest possible computer: an infinite tape of cells, a read/write head that moves one step at a time, and a table of rules. Despite its simplicity, this theoretical machine can compute anything that any modern computer can.

This simulator lets you step through classic algorithms and watch the machine work — reading symbols, writing new ones, and changing state according to its rules.

  • Choose from four algorithms: Binary Increment, Palindrome, Unary Addition, Busy Beaver
  • Step through one instruction at a time or run continuously
  • Watch the tape, state diagram, and execution log update in real time
